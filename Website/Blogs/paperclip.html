A Paperclip in A TV's Antenna Port.

Yes, I really did try to plug in a paperclip for a TV antenna. And it went horribly.

Home Blogs About Contacts A random video that you might appreciate having...?

Why did you do it?

Well, I found a YouTube video about someone connecting a paperclip into an antenna jack and then rescan for channels, and it works! So I delved into the subject and found an Antenna Man video about it!

So, where's the paperclip?

Yeah, I have a problem with this... which is I don't currently have a paperclip at all! So I just scoured it from a bag above my closet. I also found one inside an music book that I currently have.

The testing

So after I acquired the paperclip, I tried plugging in and... nothing. I tried scanning, and it didn't detect any TV channels at all. So apparently I discovered that my antenna port was a Belling-Lee port, which doesn't hold the paperclip to the port. On the opposite side is the type F port, which has a hole which you could comfortably stuff a paperclip end in it. Anyway, every single time I tried, it kept falling out the port because it didn't hold it properly!

So...

I dug under the TV's cabinet and found a TV adapter. But what was a little bit more disappointing is that this is male-to-female, which didn't help in my case as the paperclip still fell out! But I found out that our home IPTV set-top box HAS a type-F connection installed, so I unplugged the network cable, and plugged in the paperclip to the port instead. And... nothing. The thing can't rescan for channels anyway, and as it turns out, that antenna port is actually an output port instead!



ges\ So I'm going to have to try my luck on some other TVs and set-top boxes instead. Great...
